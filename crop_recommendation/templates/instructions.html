Instructions for Farmers

To accurately input the values for nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, temperature, humidity, pH, and rainfall, follow these guidelines:

  • Nitrogen (N): Measure the amount of nitrogen in your soil. This can be done using a soil test kit available at agricultural stores.
  • Phosphorus (P): Check the phosphorus level in your soil using a soil test. This nutrient is essential for root development.
  • Potassium (K): Determine the potassium content in your soil. This nutrient helps in fruit and flower development.
  • Temperature: Measure the average temperature of your growing area. You can use a thermometer or a weather app for this purpose.
  • Humidity: Record the relative humidity using a hygrometer or weather data from local sources.
  • pH: Test the pH level of your soil using a pH meter. Most crops prefer a pH level between 6 and 7.
  • Rainfall: Keep track of recent rainfall using a rain gauge or local weather reports.
